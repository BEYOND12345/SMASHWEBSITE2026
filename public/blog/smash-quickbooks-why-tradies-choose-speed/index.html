The QuickBooks Mobile Problem (That Everyone Ignores)

QuickBooks is brilliant. It's powerful, connects to everything, and your accountant loves working in it.

But QuickBooks mobile was designed for accuracy, not speed.

Here's what invoicing on-site in QB Mobile actually looks like:

Step 1: Open the app (3-5 seconds)
Step 2: Wait for sync (2-4 seconds)
Step 3: Tap Invoices (1 tap)
Step 4: Tap Create New Invoice (1 tap)
Step 5: Search for customer

  • Type customer name
  • Wait for search results
  • Tap to select
  • Average time: 20-30 seconds

Step 6: Fill in invoice details

  • Date (usually pre-filled)
  • Due date (tap calendar, scroll, select)
  • Invoice number (usually auto)

Step 7: Add line items

  • Tap to add first line item
  • Choose from service list (or create new)
  • Enter amount
  • Select tax code
  • Repeat for each line item (usually 1-3 items)

Step 8: Review and send

  • Scroll through to check
  • Tap Send
  • Choose email or SMS
  • Confirm

Total time: 10-15 minutes per invoice

You're standing in dust. WiFi is spotty. The app is laggy. You're frustrated.

And you've got 4 more jobs to do today.
