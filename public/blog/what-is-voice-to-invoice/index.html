Short answer

Voice to invoice means you describe the finished job out loud on your phone, the app turns that speech into priced line items from your catalog, you verify the lines, and you send a tax-ready PDF — usually in about 30 seconds, before you leave the driveway.

It is the opposite of Sunday-night Excel. It is also not “AI that invents prices.”

If you only need the product page, start here: Voice to invoice on iPhone.

Why the phrase matters

People search “voice to invoice” when they already know typing on a cracked screen after a long job is the bottleneck. They are not looking for another template. They want:

  1. Capture the job while details are fresh
  2. Prices that match what they actually charge
  3. A payable document out the door same visit

That is an on the job invoice — and the same loop works as an instant quote when someone asks “how much?” before you pack up.

SMASH flags unmatched items so you price them. That verify step is the product — not optional theatre.

The 30-second loop

  1. Talk — labour, materials, call-out or travel fees in plain language (often under 20 seconds of speech).
  2. Verify — check quantities and catalog prices; fix anything flagged.
  3. Send — PDF with GST where needed and a card payment link.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does voice to invoice mean?

You describe labour, materials, and fees out loud. Software builds a structured invoice from your speech and your saved rates, you verify every line, then you send a GST-ready PDF with a payment link.

How is voice to invoice different from voice notes or ChatGPT?

A voice note is unstructured text. ChatGPT drafts wording but does not know your rates or produce a payable tax invoice. Voice to invoice maps speech to your catalog and flags unknowns instead of guessing prices.

How long does voice to invoice take?

About 30 seconds end to end once your pricing is set up: a short recording, a quick verify pass, then send. Many operators finish before they start the van.

Can I use the same flow for an instant quote?

Yes. Talk the scope, verify priced lines, and send a quote on site when the customer wants a number before you leave — then convert to invoice when the job is done.

Do I still review the invoice before it sends?

Yes. Nothing sends without your check. That review step is what keeps catalog pricing honest and prevents silent AI guesses.
