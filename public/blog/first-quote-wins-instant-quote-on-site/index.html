Short answer

First quote wins. When two capable operators look the same, the customer usually goes with whoever put a clear price in their hand first — on site, same visit.

An instant quote is that number: talk the scope, verify prices from your catalog, send a PDF in about 30 seconds, before “I’ll email you later” becomes never.

Why speed to quote beats “I’ll send it tonight”

You already did the hard part — the site visit. The pain is the admin gap:

  1. Customer asks “how much?”
  2. You say you’ll send a quote
  3. You drive to the next job
  4. Someone else answers first

That is not a paperwork problem. That is a win-rate problem. A quick quote while you are still on the driveway beats a perfect spreadsheet at 9pm.

The ~30-second on-site estimate loop

  1. Talk the job — rooms, labour hours, materials, call-out or travel if it applies.
  2. Verify — check quantities and catalog prices; price anything flagged.
  3. Send — branded PDF to the customer before you leave.

That is a quick quote without rebuilding the job from memory at the kitchen table.

What “voice estimate” actually means

People say voice estimate, voice quote, or instant quote. They mean the same thing: priced lines from your rates, not a dictation note and not ChatGPT inventing dollars.

Unmatched items stay flagged until you set a price. That is how you stay accurate while still being first.

Quote today, invoice when done

When they approve and you finish the work, convert the quote to an invoice. Adjust only what changed. Send before you leave so cash follows the win — not three days later.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an instant quote on site?

A priced quote you send before you leave the visit — usually while the customer is still deciding. You describe the scope out loud, match your catalog rates, verify, and send a professional PDF.

Does the first quote really win the job?

Often yes. Customers hire the operator who gives a clear number while the problem is fresh. “I’ll email a quote later” loses to whoever replies first with a real price.

How is a voice quote different from a voice invoice?

Same capture and pricing flow. A quote is the number before the work; an invoice is the bill after. Convert when the job is done so you do not retype labour and materials.

How long does a quick quote take with SMASH?

About 30 seconds end to end once your prices are loaded: a short recording, a verify pass, then send. Desk requests can use the Gmail extension instead.

Can I turn the quote into an invoice later?

Yes. Keep the priced lines, adjust if scope changed, and send the invoice before you leave the finished job.
