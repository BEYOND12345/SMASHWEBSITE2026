# SMASH Invoices vs Typing Invoices: What 60 Seconds Really Costs You

**By Dan Reeve** — Working handyman, founder of SMASH Invoices.

If you're a sole trader invoicing 20 jobs per week and each invoice takes 5 minutes to type, you spend 100 minutes per week on invoicing admin. That's 87 hours per year — the equivalent of two and a half full working weeks spent doing data entry for money you've already earned. Voice invoicing reduces that to under 10 minutes per week. The same 20 invoices. One-tenth of the time.

---

## How long does typing an invoice actually take?

Not the optimistic version. The real version.

You get home. You open the invoicing app. You select the client — or you add them fresh if they're new. You type the job description. You add a line item. You look up the labour rate (or try to remember it). You add materials — this requires either memory or a browser lookup. You check the GST calculation. You preview. You send.

For a simple job with one service and no materials: **3–4 minutes**.
For a job with labour and 3–4 materials: **6–8 minutes**.
For a new client you've never invoiced before: **8–12 minutes**.

That's if you're not tired. If you're doing it at the end of a working day, from memory, on a phone screen with dirty fingers — add 30–50% to every estimate.

| Invoice type | Typing (per invoice) | Voice (per invoice) | Time saved |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|------------|
| Repeat job, saved client | 3–4 min | 20–30 sec | ~3 min |
| New service, existing client | 5–7 min | 35–45 sec | ~5 min |
| Multiple materials, new client | 8–12 min | 50–60 sec | ~8 min |
| Complex job with many items | 12–18 min | 60–90 sec | ~12 min |

## The real-world time cost over a year

Say you invoice 20 jobs per week. Average job: 5 minutes to type.

100 minutes per week. Every week.
86 hours and 40 minutes per year.

At an opportunity cost of $80/hour (a conservative tradesperson rate), that's **$6,933 in time spent typing invoices you've already earned**.

Voice invoicing at 40 seconds per invoice: **13 minutes per week. 11 hours per year. $880 in time.**

The difference: **75 hours saved. Over $6,000 per year.** Just from removing the typing.

---

## But time isn't the only thing typing costs you

**It costs accuracy.** You type invoices from memory, at the end of a day, when you're tired. The $50 error rate from tired invoicing compounds across the week. Typed invoices from memory are consistently less accurate than invoices generated immediately on-site.

**It costs jobs.** Every minute you spend typing is a minute you're not on the tools or quoting the next job. 87 hours per year is 87 hours of billable work you couldn't take on because you were doing admin.

**It costs consistency.** Typing requires effort, so it gets deferred. Deferred invoices become forgotten invoices. The average sole trader loses $2,400–$4,200 per year to jobs they completed and never invoiced.
