# SMASH Invoices vs Tradify — When You Don't Need a Full Platform

**By Dan Reeve** — Working handyman and founder of SMASH Invoices. Built SMASH after losing $1,200 in uninvoiced jobs in a single year. He still takes on handyman work and uses SMASH on every job. [About Dan →](https://smashinvoices.com/about)

Tradify is a full job management platform, quoting, scheduling, timesheets, team management, invoicing, and Xero/MYOB sync. It starts at roughly $50 AUD/month and is designed for trade businesses with 2–10+ employees. SMASH Invoices is a voice-first quoting and invoicing app for sole operators, it starts at $14.99/month and is designed for one person who wants to invoice in under 60 seconds before leaving a job site. These are not competing tools, they serve different businesses.

---

## The core difference

Tradify was built to manage a trades business with moving parts: multiple technicians, complex job scheduling, time tracking for payroll, progress billing, integrations.

SMASH was built for the moment after a sole operator finishes a job and wants to send a quote or invoice before driving to the next one.

If you have staff, Tradify (or similar) is the right category. If you're a sole operator and admin is the problem, not scheduling, not team management, SMASH is the right tool.

## The honest advice

If you have two or more employees, you need something like Tradify. SMASH is not the right tool, you need scheduling, timesheets, and multi-user access.

If you're a sole operator doing 5–25 jobs per week and your problem is the invoicing pile, not team coordination, Tradify is overkill. You're paying $50/month for features you'll never touch, setting up a complex system when what you need is a 30-second invoice.

I know tradies who tried Tradify because it seemed professional, found it overwhelming for a one-person operation, and gave up on software entirely. That outcome is worse than using a simpler tool that actually gets used.

## When to consider Tradify over SMASH

- You have 2+ employees and need to schedule their work
- You need timesheet tracking for payroll
- You need progress billing on large projects
- You need your invoicing to sync directly to Xero or MYOB right now
- You're scaling from sole operator to a team

## When to use SMASH over Tradify

- You're a sole operator and you invoice daily
- You want to send invoices on-site without typing
- Your problem is admin time and forgotten invoices, not team coordination
- You want to be set up and sending your first invoice within 15 minutes
- You do repeat jobs and want automatic pricing from previous work

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Tradify worth it for a sole trader?**
Tradify is feature-rich but designed for teams. For a sole trader doing 5–20 jobs per week, the complexity and cost of Tradify is often overkill. A sole trader's primary admin problem is invoice speed and consistency, which a voice-first tool addresses more directly and at lower cost.

**Does Tradify have voice invoicing?**
No. Tradify requires manual entry through its form interface. You select a client, create a job, add line items, and send. For a sole operator doing multiple jobs per day, this creates a significant end-of-day admin backlog that voice invoicing eliminates.

**Can I use SMASH Invoices and Tradify together?**
Technically yes, but it's unlikely you'd need both. If you're using Tradify for scheduling and job management and want to invoice faster on-site, SMASH could handle the invoicing side. But in most cases, sole operators choose one tool.

**Does SMASH Invoices have a free trial?**
Yes. SMASH Invoices has a free tier with full feature access limited by monthly invoice volume. You can test the entire workflow, voice input, materials catalogue, customer portal, read receipts, without a credit card.

**What's the cheapest full-featured invoicing app for sole traders in Australia?**
SMASH Invoices Starter at $14.99/month is one of the lowest-cost options with voice input, a materials catalogue, GST compliance, and a customer payment portal. Invoice2go Starter at $5.99/month is cheaper but requires typing. The right price comparison depends on whether voice input is valuable to your workflow.
