## 1. Executive Summary

SMASH Invoices is now live with a strong base of product pages, free tools, trade-specific pages, country pages, comparison pages and blog content. Google Search Console is showing early discovery signals: the site is being crawled, impressions are appearing across multiple page types, and a few high-value pages are already close to page-one visibility.

The next phase is not simply "write more blog posts." The opportunity is to build SMASH into a topical authority around one clear market position:

> **SMASH is the invoicing system for service workers who already know the job, know the price, and hate turning it into paperwork.**

This connects our product features, ICPs and SEO opportunities:

- Voice-to-invoice for people who do not want to type.
- AI invoicing for people who want the invoice generated from natural language.
- Gmail invoicing for people who quote and invoice from their inbox.
- Repeat invoicing for recurring weekly work.
- Fixed-rate service catalog / Pricing DNA for people who already know their prices.
- NDIS participant number support for compliance-heavy support workers.
- iOS app for on-the-go work.
- Chrome extension for inbox/laptop workflows.

The goal is to make Google understand SMASH as the answer for:

- voice invoicing
- AI invoice generation
- invoice app for tradies
- invoice app for cleaners
- invoice app for NDIS support workers
- Gmail invoice extension
- invoice without typing
- repeat invoice for weekly clients
- GST/tax invoice tools

## 5. The Core Strategic Concept

The content strategy should not be "invoice software content." That category is broad and competitive.

The strategy should be:

> **SMASH helps service workers get the invoice out before admin has a chance to become a problem.**

That concept can be adapted for every ICP:

- Cleaners: same clients, same services, repeat invoices.
- Plumbers: call-out fees, parts, labour, urgent jobs.
- Electricians: fixed services, compliance jobs, materials.
- Mobile mechanics: call-out charge, diagnosis, labour, parts.
- NDIS support workers: participant number, weekly shifts, compliance.
- Handymen: varied work, many small jobs, no time for admin.
- Gardeners/pool maintenance: recurring weekly/monthly work.

The SEO value comes from connecting ICPs to product features in a way that feels specific and useful.

## 8. Internal Linking Strategy

Internal links should be designed deliberately, not left to chance.

### ICP pages should link to:

- the relevant tool page
- the relevant feature page
- the relevant platform page
- 2-3 supporting blog posts
- pricing
- App Store / Chrome Store CTAs

Example for cleaners:

- `/for-cleaners`
  - links to `/voice-invoicing`
  - links to `/invoice-generator`
  - links to `/quote-generator`
  - links to `/chrome-extension`
  - links to `/customer-approval`
  - links to repeat-invoice content
  - links to cleaning invoice template/content

### Feature pages should link back to ICPs

Example for repeat invoices:

- Cleaners
- NDIS support workers
- Gardeners
- Pool maintenance
- Mobile mechanics
- Handymen

### Tool pages should link into product and ICP pages

Example for invoice generator:

- AI invoicing
- Voice invoicing
- Invoice app for cleaners
- Invoice app for tradies
- Invoice app for NDIS support workers
- Chrome extension
- iOS App Store

### Blog posts should link to:

- one parent cluster page
- one related ICP page
- one relevant tool
- one product CTA

This helps Google understand that the site is not a collection of random invoice articles; it is a connected product authority.
