# SMASH Invoices SEO & AI Search Master Plan

**Version:** 3.0  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Purpose:** Single source of truth for website SEO, content architecture, internal linking, and AI-search strategy.

This plan supersedes generic SEO work. It should be reviewed at the start of every SMASH website session.

---

## North Star

> **The fastest way for self-employed service workers to invoice — by voice.**

Every SEO decision, page, keyword, feature explanation and content asset should reinforce:

- speed
- voice-first invoicing
- self-employed service workers
- real service-business workflows

If a page does not support that, it needs a strong reason to exist.

---

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Anti-Admin

The enemy is not another invoice app. The enemy is the admin task:

- the pile of uninvoiced jobs
- the quote never sent
- the customer who says they never got it
- the material cost forgotten
- the weekly repeat job typed again

Copy should position SMASH against admin chaos, not just against competitor software.

### 2. Voice-First

Voice invoicing is the moat. SMASH should become the product Google and AI engines associate with:

- voice to invoice
- voice invoicing app
- invoice without typing
- speak an invoice
- invoice from a 30-second voice note

Protect `/voice-invoicing`. Do not change its title/meta without explicit instruction because it is already showing strong ranking signal.

### 3. Trade And Service Specific

Generic invoice software is for Xero and QuickBooks. SMASH should go deep by segment:

- cleaners
- handymen
- gardeners
- mobile mechanics
- plumbers
- electricians
- NDIS support workers
- painters
- pest control
- pool maintenance
- car detailers

Trade-specific pages are not just SEO pages. They should show how SMASH fits that worker's actual job pattern.

## Language Rules

- Use **service businesses** or **self-employed service workers** as the umbrella language.
- Use trade-specific language on ICP pages and ads.
- Avoid customer-facing technical language like GPT, Deepgram, or machine learning.
- Do not say "easy"; show the workflow instead.
- Prefer concrete timing: **under 60 seconds**, **before you leave the driveway**.
- Primary CTA is **Start Free**.
- Use feature names less than outcomes. Example: "you'll know the second they open it" is better than "read receipt functionality."

## ChatGPT Origin Story

SMASH was built because generic AI/chat tools could not reliably write real invoices for job-site work.

This is a positioning asset:

> "I tried to invoice with ChatGPT. It couldn't handle the rates, GST, customer history, and job context. So I built SMASH."

Use this carefully in:

- founder story
- FAQ
- AI invoicing content
- blog/AEO content
- social/ad hooks

Important distinction: customer-facing copy should focus on the outcome. Use ChatGPT in educational/comparison content, not as the core product promise.

Priority content:

- "Can ChatGPT write my invoices?"
- "I tried to invoice with ChatGPT. Here's why I built SMASH instead."
- "Why generic AI is not enough for tradie invoices"
- "AI invoice generator vs voice invoicing app"

The tools are not support pages. They are acquisition pages and AI-answer assets.

Current tool/product pages:

- `/gst-calculator`
- `/invoice-generator`
- `/quote-generator`
- `/invoice-template`
- `/hourly-rate-calculator`

Note: Some older planning docs used `/tools/invoice-generator` style URLs. The current website routes are root-level (`/invoice-generator`, `/quote-generator`, etc.). Do not change live indexed URLs without a redirect plan.

### Tool Funnel

1. User lands on a tool for a practical job.
2. Tool solves the problem immediately.
3. Page shows the manual pain.
4. CTA connects to SMASH doing it by voice.
5. Internal links push to ICP pages, feature pages, and pricing.

Example:

`tradie hourly rates` -> `hourly rate calculator` -> `invoice/quote generator` -> "SMASH applies your rate automatically from a voice note" -> Start Free.

## Technical SEO Checklist

Every new or materially updated page must check:

- unique title
- unique meta description
- explicit OG title/description
- canonical URL
- one H1
- answer block
- FAQ section where useful
- FAQ schema where FAQ exists
- Article schema for blog posts
- WebApplication schema for tools where appropriate
- minimum two internal links
- Start Free CTA
- Chrome/iOS CTAs where relevant
- sitemap updated for new URLs
- route added before catch-all
- mobile 375px check

---

## Content Engine Rules

Before writing more content, audit existing posts:

- word count
- duplicate primary keywords
- unique title/meta
- internal links
- schema
- whether the post belongs to a cluster

Freshness rule: update important published pages at least every 45 days with a real improvement: FAQ, stat, internal link, screenshot, date, or content section.
